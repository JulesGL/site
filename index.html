About me

Welcome to my website ! I'm Jules GOMEL, a first-year PhD Student, specializing in Neurosciences and Artificial Intelligence.

Currently, I’m conducting research at the ISAE-Supaero Neuroergonomics and Human Factors Lab, under the supervision of Dr. Frédéric Dehais. My work primarily focuses on neuromarkers of visual information integration, with the goal of advancing Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) applications, building upon the lab's research in cVEP and SSVEP.

I’m passionate about exploring the intersection of neuroscience and AI, and always open to collaborations, whether related to research or shared interests. Feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn, where I share insights on my research and related topics.

Let’s get in touch if you'd like to learn more or explore potential projects together!

Conducting Experiment on Feedback and Starburst Reactive BCI

I am currently conducting an experiment to evaluate the effects of real-time feedback on Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) performance, with a focus on the Starburst reactive BCI technique.

This research is aimed at improving BCI systems by studying the impact of feedback on user experience and performance in interactive settings.

Started My PhD

I began my PhD journey in December 2024. Excited to dive into new challenges and research in the field of Neurosciences and AI!

This project is funded by ISAE-Supaero and the French AID.

Graduated with a Master of ISAE-Supaero (Ingénieur)

I successfully graduated with a Master's degree in Neuroengineering and Signal Processing from ISAE-Supaero in 2024. It was an intense and rewarding journey, filled with challenges that helped me grow professionally and personally.

Special thanks to my professors, colleagues, and everyone who supported me along the way!
