About me

Welcome to my website ! I'm Jules GOMEL, first-year PhD Student, specializing in Neurosciences and Machine Learning after my Aeronautics Engineering Master.

Currently, I’m conducting research at the ISAE-Supaero Neuroergonomics and Human Factors Lab, under the supervision of Dr. Frédéric Dehais. My work focuses on neuromarkers of visual information processing, with the goal of advancing Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) applications, building upon the lab's research in cVEP and SSVEP.

I’m passionate about neuroscience, and always open to collaborations, whether related to research or shared interests. Feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn, where I share insights on my research and related topics.

Let’s get in touch if you'd like to learn more or explore potential projects together!

You can learn more about the ongoing experiments we have, the life of the lab or also some articles we write on the website of the lab !

Experiment on textured SSVEP and user-comfort

For my PhD, I am conducting an experiment on using textured stimuli for SSVEP BCI.

This research is aimed at improving user-comfort and signal-to-noise ratio to improve feature extraction and classification for SSVEP BCI.

Experiment on Feedback and Starburst Reactive BCI

I have conducted an experiment to evaluate the effects of real-time feedback on Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) performance, with a focus on the Starburst reactive BCI technique.

This research is aimed at improving BCI systems by studying the impact of feedback on user experience and performance in interactive settings. This research has been submitted to IEEE SMC 2025.
